Where to Stay in Las Vegas

Looking for a hotel that actually fits your trip? This is your shortlist.

We’ve filtered out the noise and highlighted hotels that deliver — based on location, comfort, style, and value. Each one is grouped by who it’s best for, so you don’t waste time digging through listings that don’t match what you need.

The Venetian Resort Las Vegas

Price per night: From $300 USD

All-suite rooms, marble finishes, and indoor gondolas set this resort apart. It’s polished, huge, and central — perfect for first-timers or couples who want the full Las Vegas experience with space to spread out.

Best for: Couples city breaks Strip-side stays

Features:

  • Indoor canal with gondola rides

  • Direct access to The Palazzo and Grand Canal Shoppes

  • 20+ restaurants and bars

  • Large casino and day spa

Circa Resort & Casino (Adults-Only)

Price per night: From $200 USD

Downtown energy meets upscale design. Circa is 21+ only, with a rooftop pool deck, huge sportsbook, and stylish rooms that give it a sharper edge than the rest.

Best for: Adults-only trips sports fans rooftop days

Features:

  • Stadium Swim rooftop complex

  • Largest sportsbook in the U.S.

  • High-floor rooms with skyline views

  • No kids, no noise

View Hotel

Bellagio Hotel & Casino

Price per night: From $320 USD

Fountain-front, timeless, and refined without being stiff. Bellagio is an iconic Strip choice for special occasions or travellers looking to slow things down without losing the Las Vegas buzz.

Best for: Couples return visitors relaxed stays

Features:

  • Famous fountain show every 15 minutes

  • Botanical garden and art exhibits

  • Fine dining (Spago, Picasso)

  • Elegant rooms, quiet atmosphere

View Hotel

Resorts World Las Vegas

Price per night: From $250 USD

Three hotels in one (Hilton, Conrad, Crockfords), all under one roof. Modern design, tech-forward features, and a top-tier food hall make this north Strip newcomer a smart pick.

Best for: Modern stays food lovers longer trips

Features:

  • 40+ bars and restaurants

  • Rooftop infinity pool and spa

  • Zouk Nightclub and Ayu Dayclub

  • Smart check-in and keyless room access

View Hotel

NoMad Las Vegas

Price per night: From $230 USD

Tucked inside Park MGM, NoMad delivers a more private, moody feel. It’s styled, calm, and a world away from the Strip’s usual noise — without actually leaving it.

Best for: Boutique-style stays couples solo travellers

Features:

  • Library-themed bar

  • Custom-designed rooms with vintage finishes

  • Private pool for NoMad guests

  • Connected to Dolby Live & Eataly

View Hotel

The Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas

Price per night: From $310 USD

Cosmo hits hard with its rooftop pool scene, buzzing restaurants, and rooms that come with actual balconies. It’s bold and loud — ideal for group trips or nightlife-focused stays.

Best for: Party weekends girls/guys trips first-timers

Features:

  • Balcony rooms with Strip views

  • Marquee Dayclub & Nightclub

  • Award-winning dining (Momofuku, é by José Andrés)

  • Art-forward interiors and bold design

View Hotel

Mandalay Bay Resort & Casino

Price per night: From $180 USD

A beach-style resort with a wave pool, lazy river, and everything you need on-site. Great for families, event-goers, or anyone who wants a slightly slower pace.

Best for: Families concerts sport weekends

Features:

  • 11-acre pool complex with sand beach

  • Shark Reef Aquarium

  • 30+ restaurants and lounges

  • Easy access to Allegiant Stadium and Luxor

View Hotel

Ellis Island Hotel, Casino & Brewery

Price per night: From $95 USD

Small, affordable, and just a few blocks off the Strip. It’s not flashy — but it’s clean, friendly, and delivers serious bang for your buck with food, drinks, and parking included.

Best for: Budget stays solo travellers short trips

Features:

  • Craft brewery, BBQ, and 24/7 café

  • Free parking

  • No long walks to the Strip

  • Very low resort fees

View Hotel

Virgin Hotels Las Vegas

Price per night: From $160 USD

Off-Strip, easygoing, and no hidden charges. Virgin offers clean design, no resort fees, and a relaxed pace that suits longer or quieter visits.

Best for: Digital nomads relaxed trips off-Strip stays

Features:

  • No resort fees, free Wi-Fi, free parking

  • Stylish rooms with tech features

  • Live music venues and casual dining

  • Calmer pool scene

View Hotel

The STRAT Hotel, Casino & Tower

Price per night: From $80 USD

Basic rooms, but with a twist — you’re staying in Vegas’ tallest building. The rides at the top and rooftop views make this budget pick feel like an experience.

Best for: First-timers budget travellers thrill seekers

Features:

  • 1,149 ft observation deck

  • SkyJump, X-Scream, and Big Shot rides

  • Rooftop pool and bar

  • On-site shows and comedy clubs
