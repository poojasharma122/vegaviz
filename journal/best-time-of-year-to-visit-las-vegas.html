Best Time of Year to Visit Las Vegas

Las Vegas is open 365 days a year — but that doesn’t mean every week is ideal. Summer heat is brutal. Winter nights can be colder than you’d expect. The sweet spot? Spring and fall.

March through May and October through early November hit the perfect balance. Pool weather is solid, crowds are manageable, and prices haven’t spiked. Weekdays offer the best hotel deals and fewer lineups across the board. Avoid major convention weeks if you’re not attending — they drive up rates citywide.

Mid-January and early December tend to be surprisingly cheap — low tourist traffic, but everything’s still running. And if you want the biggest energy? First weekends of March Madness, Halloween, and New Year’s Eve are peak chaos (in a good way).
